02
Research · Finance · Econometrics Jan – Apr 2025 · Marwadi University

Volatility Analysis of Blue-Chip Mutual Funds Using GARCH Model

Major Research Project submitted to Faculty of Management Studies, Marwadi University. Analyzed volatility of India's top 10 NSE/BSE blue-chip companies (Jan–Dec 2024) using GARCH(1,1) econometric modeling — confirming that 9 out of 10 funds exhibit high volatility with measurable risk persistence.

10 Blue-Chip Companies Studied
249+ Trading Days of Data
3 Volatility Clusters Found
  • ADF stationarity testing — all 10 price series non-stationary, confirmed stationary after 1st differencing (p < 0.05)
  • GARCH(1,1) & Heteroskedasticity models estimating Omega (ω), Alpha (α) & Beta (β) for each company
  • Granger causality tests — identified Reliance → L&T (p = 7E-14) and SBI → ICICI (p = 7E-10) as strongest causal pairs
  • K-means clustering on GARCH parameters revealed 3 behavioral groups: Shock Reactors, Calm & Stable, Balanced Movers
  • Sharpe & Treynor Ratio analysis for risk-adjusted return evaluation
Key Finding: ITC led Shock Reactors with Alpha = 0.63 (extremely strong reaction to new information). Hindustan Unilever was the most stable stock with Beta = 0.92 (high volatility memory, low reaction to shocks).
